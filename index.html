In loving memory of

December 18, 1946 to September 12, 1977

Bantu Steve Biko

    1946: Birth
  • December 18: Steve Biko is born in Ginsberg, a township outside King William's Town in the Eastern Cape, South Africa.
    • 1966: University of Natal
  • 1966: Biko enrolls in medical school at the University of Natal, where he becomes active in student politics.
    • 1968: Formation of SASO
  • 1968: Biko co-founds the South African Students' Organisation (SASO), which advocates for the rights of black students and promotes the philosophy of Black Consciousness.
    • 1972: Expulsion from Medical School
  • 1972: Due to his political activities, Biko is expelled from the University of Natal. He then becomes more deeply involved in community work and the Black Consciousness Movement.
    • 1973: Banning Order
  • March 1973: The apartheid government places a banning order on Biko, restricting his movements and prohibiting him from speaking in public or being quoted.
    • 1975: Testimony in the SASO Trial
  • May 1975: Biko testifies during the trial of nine SASO members, using the platform to promote Black Consciousness ideas and expose the injustices of apartheid.
    • 1977: Arrest and Death
  • August 18, 1977: Biko is arrested at a police roadblock and detained under the Terrorism Act.
  • September 12, 1977: Biko dies from injuries sustained during brutal torture by the police while in custody. His death becomes a rallying point for anti-apartheid activism.
